About Me

My name is Matthew Lock and I am a final year Electrical and Computer Engineering student from Cape Town.


Being a curious kid I loved exploring the mysteries of Ancient Egypt and learning all I could about those burning balls of gas in the sky. Curioisity drove my passion for building things with my hands and from an early age I started using Python programming to solve simple challenges. This ultimately lead me down my path to Engineering of which I am currently in hot pursuit ðŸ”¥


Through my time in engineering I have developed a sense of responsibility to apply my skills in an African context. I have heeded the oppertunity to take part in projects lead by Engineers Without Borders such as the Mama Africa and Playing with Solar projects, and have been involved in the development of Pixilate, an application focused on using Computer Vision to provide translations from English textbooks to African languages. I am currently involved with the development of an application which aims to strenghen the second hand clothing communities present on social media platforms such as Instragram
